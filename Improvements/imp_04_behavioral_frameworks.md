# Improvement Area 4: Strategic Narrative & Behavioral Frameworks

## The Objective
To provide Hexamind with a "Library of Mental Models" that allows its agents to categorize and explain raw data using established business and psychological frameworks (e.g., Maslow, Veblen, Hick's Law).

## Why this is necessary
Gemini didn't just explain $60$Hz screens. It explained **Choice Architecture** (the Decoy Effect) and **Veblen Goods** (Psychological prestige). This "Strategic Vocabulary" makes the AI feel like a C-Suite consultant rather than a search engine.

## Maximization Strategy: "Cognitive Anchoring"

### 1. The Framework Library (RAG-Anchor)
Inject a "Playbook of Strategic Frameworks" into the system prompt of the `Analyst` and `Synthesiser`. This playbook includes concise definitions of:
- **Veblen Goods** (Luxury demand logic).
- **The Paradox of Choice** (Choice Architecture).
- **Wait-and-Watch Innovation** (First-mover disadvantage).
- **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)**.

### 2. Mandatory Framework Mapping
The `Analyst` must attempt to map at least one "Behavioral Framework" to the findings. 
- *Example:* If researching a failed startup, the Analyst should look for "Sunk Cost Fallacy" or "Market Fit Gap."

### 3. Aspirational Formatting
The `Synthesiser` will be instructed to use "Thematic Headings" that reflect these frameworks (e.g., "The Psychology of Retention" instead of just "Customer Loyalty").

## Expected Outcome
Reports that offer "Aha!" moments by connecting raw news/data to universal human behaviors and economic laws.
