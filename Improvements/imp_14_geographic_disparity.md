# Improvement Area 14: Geographic Disparity Analysis

## The Objective
To pivot Hexamind from a "US-Centric" bias to a "Localized Market Intelligence" engine by specifically identifying and analyzing differences in brand/technology adoption across major global regions.

## Why this is necessary
The Gemini report included a dedicated **"Smartphone Brand Retention and Market Share (Selected Regions)"** table (Line 59-66). It showed:
- Apple thrives in the US/Japan (Retention ~90%).
- Apple faces a steeper climb in Germany (Retention 72%, below Samsung's 80%).

Without this, a report implies that success is uniform when it is actually localized and context-dependent.

## Maximization Strategy: "The Global Surveyor"

### 1. Mandatory Regional Pass
Update the `Historian` or `Researcher` to include a query for "Regional Adoption Variance."
- *Query:* "How does [Topic] adoption in the EU/Asia compare to the US? Find regional market share data."

### 2. The Cultural Blocker Check
The `Analyst` must identify "Local Peculiarities" (e.g., German sensitivity to data privacy/Right to Repair, Japanese preference for specific aesthetic designs).

### 3. Localization Data Table
The `Synthesiser` will be prompted to create a **"Global Adoption Matrix"** (Modeled after Table 1 in Gemini's report), allowing the user to see where the product is a "Dominant Leader" vs. a "Challenger."

## Expected Outcome
Reports that offer "True Global Intelligence." The user won't just learn about a trend; they will understand why it might work in London but fail in Tokyo.
