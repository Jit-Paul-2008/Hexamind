# Improvement Area 7: Long-Term Financial Modeling (TCO/ROI)

## The Objective
To move Hexamind beyond "current state" reporting and enable it to project the **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)** and **Return on Investment (ROI)** over 3, 5, and 10-year horizons.

## Why this is necessary
Gemini devoted a specific section to "Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) and Resale Value." It looked at:
- How much a Mac saves a company over 3 years ($273-$543 per unit).
- The "effective cost" of an iPhone after factoring in its 69% resale value.

This turns a "purchase" discussion into a "financial investment" discussion.

## Maximization Strategy: "The Fiscal Lens"

### 1. TCO Calculator Logic
Inject a "Financial Projection Agent" (a specialized version of the `Analyst`) that searches for:
- Initial Purchase Price.
- Maintenance/Support Costs.
- Expected Lifespan.
- Residual (Resale) Value.

### 2. Pro-Active ROI Mapping
If the subject of research has a cost (e.g., a software tool, a car, a schooling system), the `Analyst` must attempt to find data on the "Outcome" (Expected earnings increase, time saved, efficiency gain) to calculate a rough ROI.

### 3. Lifecycle Visualizations
Update the `Synthesiser` to include a heading: **"Fiscal Lifecycle & Value Retention."** This section will explicitly state if the topic is a "Depreciating Asset" or a "Stable Investment."

## Expected Outcome
Reports that provide actual "Bottom Line" value. The user won't just learn about a topic; they will understand the financial implications of sticking with it (or switching from it) over a decade.
