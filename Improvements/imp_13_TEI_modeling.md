# Improvement Area 13: Total Economic Impact (TEI) Modeling

## The Objective
To enable Hexamind to quantify the "Million Dollar Impact" of a research topic on a composite organization, using the **Forrester TEI Framework** as a baseline for fiscal analysis.

## Why this is necessary
The Gemini report specifically cites a **Forrester Research study (line 121)** which estimated that IBM saved $273 to $543 per Mac compared to PCs. This "Unit-Level ROI" is what transforms a report from "Interesting" to "Actionable Corporate Intelligence."

## Maximization Strategy: "The Fiscal Expert"

### 1. The TEI Persona Pass
Equip the `Analyst` with a specialized "Total Economic Impact" module.
- *Task:* "Research cost-of-deployment, support overhead savings, and productivity gains (TEI factors) for [Subject]."

### 2. Composite Organization Modeling
The `Analyst` will be prompted to create a hypothetical "Composite Organization" (e.g., a company of 1,000 employees) and apply the discovered data to it.
- *Calculation:* "If [Subject] saves $200/year in support, a 1,000-person organization saves $200k/year."

### 3. ROI Header
The final report will include a dedicated **"Total Economic Impact Assessment"** section, which provides a bulleted list of "Soft" vs "Hard" cost savings, mimicking the structure of a Forrester whitepaper.

## Expected Outcome
Reports that "Sell Themselves." By providing a concrete dollar value (even as an estimation), Hexamind becomes a tool for CFOs and decision-makers who care most about the bottom line.
